# Site Brief: Yardwise

Prepared for: Claude Design
Prepared by: Trevor Spencer
Date: June 19, 2026

## 1. Project Summary

A fully custom website for Yardwise, a landscaping, lawn maintenance, and smart yard technology company headquartered in Sacramento, California. The site's job is to generate leads and make it effortless for homeowners to request a quote or estimate — by phone, contact form, or quote request flow. There is no e-commerce, checkout, or online payment functionality anywhere on this site; every job is quoted and scheduled directly with the team.

## 2. The Business

Yardwise is run by Sean, a 25-year-old owner who built the company around a simple idea: yards deserve the same precision and tech that everything else in someone's life already gets. Sean runs the business alongside a small, tight crew of guys who handle the maintenance trucks, lawn care, and installs — landscape design and install, ongoing maintenance, irrigation and drip line systems, and smart device installs (irrigation controllers, soil sensors, automated lighting). It's a small operation that moves fast and does sharp, modern work.

Key facts to weave throughout the site:

- Founder-led — Sean started and runs the company personally, with a small core team of guys he trusts running the trucks and jobs day to day. Not a franchise, not a call center.
- Headquartered in Sacramento, California.
- Service area: Sacramento and surrounding communities (placeholder — confirm exact cities/counties with client, e.g. Elk Grove, Folsom, Roseville, Davis).
- Full-service: landscape design, installation, ongoing lawn care and maintenance, irrigation/drip line systems, and smart device installs and management.
- Technology-forward without being gimmicky — smart irrigation controllers with app-based zone control, soil moisture sensors for data-driven watering, and smart outdoor lighting automation, installed and set up using established platforms (e.g. Rachio, Hunter Hydrawise — confirm exact brand partnerships with client) rather than anything custom-built.
- Young, sharp, modern energy — this isn't an old-school lawn crew; it's a small team that treats yard care like a craft and uses better tools to do it.

## 3. Brand Personality & Tone of Voice

The brand should feel professional, sharp, and confidently modern — a small team that's serious about the work and smart about how they do it. Think: a crew young enough to know the tech and disciplined enough that the work always looks clean.

Tone guidelines for the copywriter:

- Confident and direct, never cutesy or gimmicky. Sharp, clean sentences over personality-heavy copy.
- Technology should read as a quiet advantage, not the headline — mention smart irrigation, sensors, and app control as part of how the work gets done well, not as the entire pitch. No more than light, occasional "optimization" or "data-driven" language; never lean into a sci-fi or startup voice.
- Credible and capable — copy can note Sean built the company and leads the crew personally, without making age the focal point or a gimmick.
- Clean, modern, no dad-joke or folksy small-talk voice (this is a deliberate contrast to a more old-school local trade brand).
- Avoid: jargon-heavy tech-speak, generic "we love what we do" filler, anything that reads like a big national lawn-care franchise.

## 4. Visual Identity

- Color palette: green and yellow as primary brand colors. Lean toward a fresher, more saturated green (not muted/earthy) paired with a clean yellow accent, plus a neutral dark charcoal or near-black for text and contrast — keep it modern rather than rustic.
- Overall feel: clean, sharp, modern — more like a well-designed local service-tech brand than a traditional landscaping company. Confident whitespace, clean grid layouts.
- Imagery: real photos of Sean and the crew on job sites, trucks, completed yards, and smart device installs where possible (placeholders to be flagged for client photos); avoid soft/rustic stock photography — keep imagery crisp and well-lit.
- Iconography: clean, modern line icons for the services grid (sprinkler head, leaf, control panel/app, lighting) — simple geometric style rather than hand-drawn or whimsical.
- Logo direction (concept stage): wordmark "Yardwise" set in green; a simple yellow icon mark pairs with it — a minimalist push-mower silhouette with a small wifi/signal accent off the handle, tying landscaping and tech together without being literal. Treat as a placeholder concept for early design review, not a final logo file.

## 5. Core Site Functionality

The site must be fully responsive and mobile-optimized — homeowners will be finding and reaching out to Yardwise from their phones, often while standing in their own yard. The three core conversion actions are:

1. Call the business — a click-to-call phone number prominently placed in the header/nav on every page, plus a sticky call button on mobile.
2. Request a quote — a quote request form (name, phone, email, address/city, property size if known, project type/services interested in) routed to the business email/CRM. Should feel fast and modern, not like a long intake form.
3. Schedule a walkthrough — a simple scheduling flow that lets homeowners choose between an in-person walkthrough (someone comes to look at the property) or a virtual walkthrough (video call walk-through of the yard) before quoting. Present both as equally valid options, not virtual-as-fallback — some homeowners will prefer to skip the wait for an in-person visit.

All three actions should be reachable from the homepage above the fold and repeated as CTAs throughout the site. No payment processing, checkout, or pricing display anywhere on the site.

## 7. Page-by-Page Content Direction

### Home
- Hero with headline, subhead, and the three primary CTAs (Call, Request a Quote, Schedule a Walkthrough). Headline should lead with capability and precision, not the tech angle (e.g. something like "Sharp landscaping, smarter yards" rather than leading with "smart home" language).
- Quick trust bar: founder-led, Sacramento-based, full-service (design through maintenance), smart system installs.
- Services overview grid (see Section 6 subpages).
- Short "meet the team" teaser linking to the About page — introduce Sean and the crew without over-emphasizing his age as a gimmick.
- Smart yard tech teaser — one focused section on irrigation controllers, soil sensors, and lighting automation, framed as "we also handle the tech side" rather than a separate product line.
- Service area teaser (map or list of cities) linking to the Service Area page.
- Testimonials/reviews strip if available.
- Closing CTA section.

### About / Our Story
- Sean's story — why he started Yardwise, what he saw missing in typical lawn/landscape companies, how the crew came together.
- Photos of Sean and the team on job sites.
- Credentials/licensing stated clearly and confidently (California landscaping contractor license — confirm license number/type with client before launch).
- Keep tone sharp and confident, not "feel-good small business" heavy — this page should build trust through competence, not nostalgia.

### Services
No checkout, no pricing — a clean, confident overview of what they handle, organized into clear categories:

- Landscape design & installation (new beds, hardscape, planting plans, sod/turf install)
- Lawn care & maintenance (mowing, edging, seasonal cleanup, fertilization)
- Irrigation & drip line systems (design, install, repair)
- Smart device installs & management (app-controlled irrigation zones, soil moisture sensors for data-driven watering schedules, smart outdoor lighting automation). Copy here should make clear this isn't a one-time install — Yardwise also manages and monitors the systems on an ongoing basis (adjusting schedules, troubleshooting, keeping things running) rather than installing and walking away. Installed on established platforms; reference specific brand partnerships once confirmed with client.

Each service gets a short, sharp description (1–2 sentences). End the section with a CTA to call, request a quote, or schedule a walkthrough.

### Service Area
- Headline confirming Yardwise is Sacramento-based and serves the surrounding region.
- Optional map graphic or list of cities/counties served.
- Keep this page tight and functional, consistent with the brand's no-fluff tone.

### Contact / Request a Quote
- Click-to-call number front and center.
- Quote request form (per Section 5).
- Schedule a walkthrough flow with a clear choice between in-person and virtual (per Section 5) — present as two simple options (e.g. two tappable cards), not a dropdown buried in a form.
- Business hours, service area reminder, and Sacramento HQ location.

## 8. SEO & Local Search Notes

- Primary local keywords should center on "landscaping Sacramento CA," "lawn care Sacramento," and "smart irrigation installation Sacramento," plus service-specific terms (e.g., "drip line installation Sacramento," "soil moisture sensor install").
- Recommend setting up/confirming a Google Business Profile and embedding consistent NAP (Name, Address, Phone) info across the site footer and Contact page.
