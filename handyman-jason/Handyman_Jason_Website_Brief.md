# Website Brief: Handyman Jason

**Location:** Sedona, Arizona
**Business type:** Independent, full-service residential handyman (mobile — comes to the client's home)
**Site type:** Single-page, mobile-first, responsive marketing site
**Prepared:** June 19, 2026

---

## 1. Project Summary

Handyman Jason needs a fast, friendly, mobile-first website that turns local searchers into calls and quote requests. Most visitors will find the site on a phone while standing in front of a leaky faucet or a broken fence — the design needs to get them to "call" or "send a quote request" in seconds, while still feeling warm, personal, and trustworthy rather than like a faceless contractor directory.

**Primary goal:** drive phone calls and quote-form submissions, weighted equally.
**Secondary goal:** build local trust and credibility (Sedona-specific, personable, reliable).

---

## 2. Brand & Visual Direction

### Color palette
Pulled from the attached Southwestern beaded medallion — a palette that's warm, earthy, and distinctly tied to Sedona's red-rock, turquoise-sky landscape. This also visually differentiates Jason from the generic blue-and-orange "handyman app" look most competitors use.

| Role | Color | Hex (approx.) | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Primary accent | Turquoise | `#2E9C95` | Header accents, links, icons — fresh, trustworthy, Sedona-coded |
| CTA / buttons | Amber Gold | `#C8862E` | "Call Now" / "Get a Quote" buttons — warm, high-contrast, action-oriented |
| Secondary accent | Deep Rust/Maroon | `#7C2530` | Section dividers, headline highlights, badges |
| Text / dark base | Charcoal | `#211B18` | Body copy, footer background |
| Background / neutral | Warm Sand | `#F2E8D8` | Page background, card fills — keeps the earthy tone without feeling heavy |
| Subtle accent | Warm Gray | `#A9A29A` | Borders, dividers, muted text |

Rule of thumb: sand/cream for backgrounds, charcoal for body text, turquoise for trust/identity elements, amber gold reserved almost exclusively for buttons and CTAs so it keeps its "click me" power.

### Typography
- **Headings:** Fraunces or Poppins (Google Fonts, free) — both have enough character to feel hand-crafted and warm without sacrificing legibility on mobile.
- **Body text:** Inter or Nunito Sans (Google Fonts, free) — clean, highly legible at small sizes, renders consistently across devices.
- Minimum body size: 16px (mobile), to avoid auto-zoom and keep readability without pinch-zooming.

### Imagery & tone
- Photos of Jason at work (real photos once available) > stock photography. Until then, use warm, sunlit, Southwestern-toned placeholder imagery (red rock backdrops, toolbelt/toolbox close-ups) — clearly marked as placeholders to be swapped before launch.
- Voice: friendly, plain-spoken, neighborly — "I'll come fix it" not "We provide comprehensive facility maintenance solutions."
- Logo: none exists yet. Recommend a simple wordmark ("Handyman Jason") paired with a small line-art icon (wrench, toolbox, or house) in amber or turquoise. Can be designed in the next session.

## 3. Site Structure (Single Page, Anchor Navigation)

A one-pager keeps things simple for a solo operator and gets everything in front of mobile visitors without extra taps. Sticky header nav scrolls to each section.

1. **Sticky Header**
   Logo/wordmark, anchor links (Services / Gallery / Reviews / Contact), persistent "Call Now" button (always visible, top-right on desktop, full-width sticky bar on mobile).

2. **Hero**
   Headline + one-line value prop, photo of Jason or his work, two equal-weight CTAs: **Call/Text Now** (tel: link) and **Get a Free Quote** (scrolls to form). Trust microcopy under CTAs (e.g., "Serving Sedona & the Verde Valley").

3. **Services**
   Icon grid of core handyman services (repairs, installs, painting, drywall, fixture replacement, minor carpentry, honey-do lists, etc.) under a "Full-Service Handyman" framing — broad enough to not turn away odd jobs, specific enough to build confidence.

4. **Why Handyman Jason** (trust signals)
   Short bullets: local to Sedona, upfront pricing, reliable scheduling, years of experience, satisfaction-focused. (Insurance/license info to be added once confirmed.)

5. **Gallery**
   Before/after or completed-job photo grid. Placeholder grid with clear "swap before launch" labeling until real photos exist.

6. **Reviews/Testimonials**
   Card layout for 3–5 quotes + star rating. Placeholder sample quotes until real reviews are gathered (e.g., from Google).

7. **Service Area**
   Simple map or list of towns served (Sedona + surrounding Verde Valley communities) — reinforces "local, not a faceless app."

8. **Quote Request Form + Contact**
   Fields: Name, Phone, Email, Service Needed (dropdown), Message, optional photo upload. Click-to-call number repeated alongside the form. Form submissions email directly to Jason.

9. **Footer**
   Phone, email, service area, hours, social links (if any), copyright. Repeat of Call Now button.

## 4. Functional Requirements

- **Click-to-call everywhere it matters**: header, hero, footer, and a fixed bottom mobile call bar (since most traffic will be on phones).
- **Quote form** routes to Jason's email/SMS; minimal required fields to reduce drop-off.
- **No online booking/scheduler** for this version — calls and quote requests are the conversion paths.
- **Fast load**: compressed/WebP images, lazy-loaded gallery, no heavy frameworks needed for a one-pager.
- **Fully responsive**: mobile (< 480px), tablet (481–768px), desktop (> 768px) breakpoints; touch targets ≥ 44px.
- **Local SEO basics**: descriptive title/meta tags, `LocalBusiness` schema markup, NAP (name/address/phone) consistency, Google Business Profile link.
- **Accessibility (WCAG AA)**: color contrast checked against the palette above (charcoal-on-sand passes easily; verify turquoise/amber text-on-background combos), descriptive alt text on all images, readable font sizes, visible focus states.
- **Analytics**: Google Analytics + Search Console hookup for tracking calls/form conversions.
